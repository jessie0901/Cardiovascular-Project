Why should you care

Cardiovascular diseases are the cause behind the highest number of deaths in Australia

Coronary heart disease killed 17,533 Australians in 2018 — or about 48 every day.

1.1 million hospitalisations were associated with CVD in 2016–17 which is 11% of all hospitalisations in Australia.

CVD death rates increased with age, with over half (53%) of CVD deaths occurring in persons aged 85 years and over in 2017.

More than 1 in 4 deaths were due to cardiovascular disease in 2017.

Indigenous Australians have CVD hospitalisation and death rates almost twice the rate of non-Indigenous Australians.

  

Prevalence of coronary heart disease was around twice as high among men (3.8%) than women (1.9%) in 2017-18.

CVD hospitalisation and death rates are around 35% higher in remote areas than major cities.

More than 4 in 5 cardiovascular disease hospitalisations occurred in those aged 55 or over.

CVD accounted for almost 14% of Australia's total burden of disease in 2015.
